Key findings for the expert panel

  • Six competencies are universally confirmed - 1.3, 2.1, 4.1, 5.2, 5.7, and 6.6 achieve strong endorsement across all five disciplines. These should be presented as settled and not subject to deliberation.
  • Five competencies require structural decisions - 5.1, 5.6, 6.2, 6.4, and 6.5 show consistent cross-disciplinary rejection at 20–31%. These are framework design issues, not wording issues.
  • Domains 1–4 are robustly validated - no meaningful disciplinary fault lines. The core of the framework is cross-disciplinary in exactly the way intended.
  • Domains 5 and 6 require panel decisions that go beyond wording revision - both show ~27% overreach and significant overlap concerns across all disciplines.
  • Education is the most distinctive voice - highest acceptance of Domain 6, unique relevance median of 7 for 6.1 and 6.2, highest merger demand in Domain 5 (38.9%). Weight their perspective heavily in both discussions.
  • Medicine is systematically the most sceptical group - highest overreach concern in D2 (28.1%), D3 (24.6%), D5 (29.8%), D6 (32%). This reflects discipline-specific curriculum constraints rather than opposition to the framework.
  • Achievability is consistently median 4 across all domains and all disciplines - the framework will require moderate curriculum development. This is a stable, cross-disciplinary signal requiring institutional implementation support.
